J.R.R Tolkien

J.R.R Tolkien was and continues to be a major influence on the fantasy genre today including many tropes that originated with Tolkien rin most fantasy literature.

    History

  • 3 January 1892
    -Birth of John Ronald Reuel Tolkien in Bloemfontein, Orange Free State (now part of South Africa).
  • October 1911
    -Tolkien begins studying at Oxford.
  • 24 September 1914
    -Tolkien writes his first identifiable “Middle-earth” fragment ‘The Voyage of Éarendel the Evening Star’.
  • March 1916
    -Tolkein marries wife Edtith and is soon dispatched to Otley during world war 1
  • 1 July 1916
    -Battle of the Somme begins. Rob Gilson, close friend and member of the T.C.B.S. dies.
  • 9 November 1916
    -Suffering from trench fever, Tolkien returns to England.
  • November 1918
    -Tolkiens return to Oxford. Tolkien obtains employment with the New English Dictionary [Oxford English Dictionary]. Tolkien probably begins work on the first version of ‘The Music of the Ainur’.
  • Autumn 1920
    -He begins work as a Reader in English Language at Leeds University. Michael, the Tolkiens’ second son is born on 22 October.
  • Autumn 1925
    -Tolkien takes up an appointment as Rawlinson and Bosworth Professor of Anglo-Saxon at Oxford. He purchases a house in Oxford.
  • 21 September 1937
    -The Hobbit is published. Because of its success, Stanley Unwin subsequently urges Tolkien to write a sequel, which he begins. This is the germ of The Lord of the Rings.
  • 23 June 1945
    -Tolkien becomes Oxford’s Merton Professor of English Language and Literature.
  • 4 December 1947
    -On Fairy-Stories is published in Essays Presented to Charles Williams.
  • 20 October 1949
    -Farmer Giles of Ham is published.
  • 29 July 1954
    -Publication of The Fellowship of the Ring.
  • 11 November 1954
    -Publication of The Two Towers.
  • 20 October 1955
    -Publication of The Return of the King.
  • 22 November 1962
    -The Adventures of Tom Bombadil is published.
  • 29 November 1971
    -Death of Edith Tolkien.
  • 2 September 1973
    -Death of J.R.R. Tolkien from a stomach ulcer.
